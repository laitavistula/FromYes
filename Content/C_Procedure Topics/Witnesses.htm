Witnesses

At any wedding ceremony in Poland, two witnesses are required. Each witness must be at least 18 years old.

  • Civil and Concordat Weddings:

    • Witnesses must have a valid identification document (e.g., national ID or passport).

  • Church Weddings:

    • While tradition suggests choosing religious witnesses of different genders as role models for the couple, this is not a strict requirement.

    • Witnesses must be at least 18 years old and have valid identification.

    • Witnesses, along with the couple, will sign the marriage certificate, typically in the sacristy before the ceremony.

    • It is traditional for witnesses to be able to participate in confession and communion, but it's not mandatory.

    • Witnesses do not need to be of the same denomination as the church of the wedding or have received confirmation.

  • Humanist Weddings:

    • Witnesses are not legally required at the actual ceremony, as the couple signs the marriage certificate at the civil ceremony at the Register office (Urząd Stanu Cywilnego, USC). However, couples may choose to have witnesses for symbolic purposes.
