Civil ceremony requirements

Required documents:

  • Valid identification (passport or national ID)

  • Birth certificates

  • A certificate confirming the legal capacity to marry issued by the appropriate authority in one’s home country. If obtaining this document is not possible, a Polish court's decision to waive this requirement is necessary.

  • Proof of termination of previous marriages (divorce or death certificate)

  • Proof of address in Poland (if applicable)

Important: All foreign documents must be translated into Polish by a sworn translator.

Process:

  1. Apply at the local registry office (Urząd Stanu Cywilnego - USC) at least 30 days before the wedding. Note that the marriage can take place no sooner than one month after submitting the declaration of no impediment to marriage.

  2. Pay the application fee (via bank transfer, payment machine, or cash in Polish złoty).

  3. Both partners must be present and sign the marriage intent declaration.

Marriage Ceremony:

  • Civil ceremonies can take place at the registry office or other approved venues.

  • Additional requirements and fees may apply for non-office locations.
