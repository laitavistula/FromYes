Rings

Within the framework of Polish marriage customs, the exchange of wedding rings constitutes a significant element of both religious and civil ceremonies.

Procedural details:

  • Civil marriages: the exchange of rings is an optional procedural element, subject to the couple's preference.

  • Church marriages: ring exchange is a mandatory procedural step, with the rings undergoing benediction by the officiating priest.

  • Traditional practice: the established norm in Poland dictates that both spouses wear the wedding ring on the third digit of the right hand.

  • Ring presentation protocol: the rings are frequently presented by a designated witness or, in religious settings, a liturgical minister (e.g., ministrant).

  • Legal imperative: it is crucial to note that the act of exchanging rings in a civil ceremony does not constitute a prerequisite for the legal validation of the marital union.

  • Procurement timeline: to ensure timely availability, it is recommended that wedding rings be ordered a minimum of 2-3 months prior to the scheduled wedding date.
